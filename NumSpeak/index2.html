The + and - symbols: These are prefixed onto "words" (x*y) to change the meaning. Usually it means good/bad or yes/no respectively, but it could mean other things.
Some words have a necessary + or - in front (Ex: 4 or Î”*5). These words are highlighted yellow in the previous chart; a + makes it the left side (of the /) while a - makes it the right side.


Parentheses are usually a grouping symbol, but it depends on how many there are:

(x)((x))(((x)))

With this, you can write a full sentence! NumberSpeak has English punctuation (ignore factorials and decimals, they don't exist.) and Toki Pona-like grammar. Multiplication must be grouped, so you can have a sentence like so: 1(0*7)(2*3). which means "I see a rainbow".
